    We’re not kitten you. Us Weekly tried kitten yoga and it was everything we wanted and more. From downward-facing cat to the cat-cow pose, Us Weekly video correspondent Christina Garibaldi and Senior Entertainment Editor Jamie Blynn rolled out their yoga mats and tried it all. Paws right there and watch the video above to see how they did!

    The class was held on April 12 to celebrate not only Best Friends Adoption Center’s one-year anniversary in New York City, but also to raise awareness about kitten season, which lasts from February through November when animal shelters are inundated by homeless and orphaned kitties who are looking for a home.

    Yoga instructor Christina Gdisis, who is also the foster coordinator at the center, instructed the hour-long sesh while adoptable kittens climbed on backs, pounced from one person to the next and even tried out their own cat poses.

    So how did we do? It wasn’t a total cat-astrophe! “It was so fun! I’m never doing an exercise without animals,” Blynn said.

    “It makes everything so much better,” Garibaldi agreed. “It was very relaxing, I’m not very good at yoga, but the cats made it so much better!”
