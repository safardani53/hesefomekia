    A PRISONER broke his leg when he scaled 25ft high walls in a daring escape from Pentonville prison - with the escapee dying his ginger hair black in an attempt to avoid capture.

    Matt Baker was in custody awaiting sentencing for attempted murder when he made the "Alcatraz" style escape - with the 28-year-old allegedly found hiding under his sister's bed.

    Kelly Baker, 21, now stands accused of buying hair-dye to hide her brother's distinctive red hair and hiding him in her home.

    She appeared in court, charged with aiding an offender after her brother arrived at her home in Ilford, 12 miles east of the Pentonville prison, with a broken leg and threatening suicide.

    It is alleged that the woman helped her brother hide, with Baker having been convicted of attempted murder a fortnight ago for stabbing Matthew Bennett 25 times at a house part in Dagenham, east London, last year.

    Baker had escaped from the North London prison on Monday night, along with inmate James Whitlock, with the pair using diamond-tipped cutting equipment to cut through the cell bar.

    The cunning pair also left clothing and pillows in their beds to fool the prison guards.

    Ms Baker faced Highbury Corner Magistrates this morning, with the red-haired woman appearing in custody wearing light blue jeans and a grey hoodie, only speaking to confirm her name and address.

    Officers from the Met's Flying Squad and Homicide and Major Crime Command are hunting the remaining lag.

    The missing inmate is described as a white man of slim build and has the word 'Tracy' tattooed on his torso.

    People have been urged not approach Whitlock and to call 999 if he is seen.

    The running of Pentonville came under intense criticism following the embarrassing admission that inmates had escaped.

    Steve Gillian, the general secretary of the Prison Officers Association, has described Britain's prisons as "in crisis" and claims that 10,000 uniformed staff have been lost since 2010.
