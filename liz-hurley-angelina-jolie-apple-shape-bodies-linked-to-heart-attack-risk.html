    WOMEN with apple-shaped bodies are more likely to have heart attacks, a study reveals.

    Having a less defined waist poses a greater threat than having bigger hips, legs or bum.

    It means celebrities such as Liz Hurley and ­Catherine Zeta-Jones are at increased risk of harm.

    Researchers from the University of Oxford analysed data on nearly 500,000 UK adults aged 40 to 69.

    The overweight suffered more heart attacks over a seven-year period than those of a healthy size.

    But people with a high waist to hip ratio — having a thick middle — were at greatest risk.

    Each 5.2- point rise in BMI increased the likelihood of a woman suffering a heart attack by 22 per cent.

    The risk rose more as they got less pear-shaped. Around 14 per cent of women are apple-shaped.

    MOST READ IN HEALTH

    Dr Sanne Peters said: “Having proportionally more fat around the abdomen appears more hazardous.”

    The British Heart Foundation said: “Women are often misdiagnosed initially when having a heart attack so body shape may be an important factor for medical staff to consider.”
