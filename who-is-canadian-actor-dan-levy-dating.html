    Who is Dan Levy?

    Born August 9, 1983, Daniel Levy is a Canadian actor, writer, director and producer.

    Levy began his career in television as the host of MTV Canada in 2006.

    He gained attention as the co-host of MTV Canada's The After Show and it various replicas, such as The Hills: The After Show and The City: Live After Show.

    Following The After Show's cancellation, Levy wrote, produced and starred in his own Christmas Special for MTV, Daniel Levy's Holi-Do's & Don't's.

    In 2013, Levy and his father Eugene Levy began writing CBC's Schitt's Creek.

    For his work on Schitt's Creek, Levy was nominated for numerous awards, including several Canadian Screen Awards for writing and acting, winning the awards for Best Comedy Series, Best Writing in a Comedy Program, and Best Comedy Series.

    In 2019, the series was nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series.

    In March 2019, Levy announced that the series was renewed for a sixth and final season, and said the decision to end the series after season six on their own creative terms was a "rare privilege".

    Who is Dan Levy dating?

    Dan Levy is not currently dating anyone, in 2015, Levy discussed his dating status in an interview with Out Magazine.

    He revealed that his busy TV schedule hindered his ability to focus on his love life.

    “I've been single for a bit. It's a strange thing,” Levy said at the time.

    “I don't know whether it's an excuse that I'm telling myself or whether it's just what it is, but I feel like this job has consumed my life.

    “I hope that I can find someone when it's all done, or when things are a little bit more smooth,” Levy continued.

    “But the eye has really been on making sure that the show is as good as it can be, so that I can be employed for another year.

    “When the time comes and all the stars are aligned, someone else will come in and hopefully we'll call it a day in the love life department."

    "I believe it was mom who just actually said, 'OK, are you gay?'" added Eugene.

    "She did. My mom asked me over for lunch one day and I said, 'Yes,'" recalled Levy.
