    FANS of Little People, Big World Little People have slammed Audrey & Jeremy Roloff’s lavish Christmas decorations and appear to favor Tori and Zach’s classy display.

    With the holiday season in full swing, members of the Roloff family are busy decorating their homes and setting up their trees.

    While Tori and Zach have been praised for their simple, yet elegant decor in their new home, siblings Audrey and Jeremy have been slammed for going too far over the top.

    Not only was she attacked over the sheer size of her and her husband's tree, which barely fit in their living room, but for how she decorated it.

    Days before Christmas, the author shared a photo of her fully done-up Christmas tree, capturing the many lights, ornaments, and the topper.

    Fans ripped the decor as "ugly," and appeared to take particular issue with her tree topper.

    Audrey opted for a massive, and seemingly somewhat asymmetrical star to cap off her huge tree.

    The star is yellow with lights outlining the shape.

    She showed it off in a social media post which showed her husband Jeremy standing proudly in front of the tree with his arms outstretched.

    Her caption read: "Big time thank you to this guy."

    She went on: "Who is a constant display of 'if there's a will, there's a way.'"

    Audrey concluded: "@jeremyroloff I love you."

    FAN'S REACT

    A fan grabbed a photo of the post and shared it on a popular Reddit board dedicated to the long-running TLC show.

    The Redditor titled the post: "I feel like if their life wasn’t on display on Instagram 24/7 he wouldn’t be doing what he does."

    In the comment thread, one person wrote: "I agree... it's a hill I'll die on, that people [who] feel the need to constantly brag about their spouses are actually miserable."

    Another fan commented: "Exactly it's like everything is InTeNtIoNaL," with a third adding, "And VINTAGE."

    A fourth commenter mentioned: "I can’t imagine how many bugs and rodents were in that tree. Ick. Hope to never see this hodgepodge of [a] tree again."

    TORI'S TREE

    When it came time for an authentic tree, Tori and Zach went all out - finding and cutting down their own holiday pine.

    While fans applauded them for being far more subtle with their tree choice, they went big in other areas.

    After Tori showed off their train set under the tree, fans speculated that the star might be trying to outdo her sister-in-law.

    Hours after Audrey posted a photo captioned "watching the train," that showed herself and Jeremy's middle child, two-year-old Bode, watching the toy train circle around the tree, Tori took to social media to share a similar post.

    The next post from Audrey showed the train in motion as it traveled around gifts piled at the bottom. 

    Audrey explained: "@jeremyroloff fixed the train he had growing up."

    A few hours later, Tori shared several toy train posts of her own on Instagram.

    First, she recorded her and Zach's sons Jackson and Josiah playing with the train set.

    An excited Josiah repeatedly rant into the tracks with his walker unknowingly as Jackson hurried to fix the broken links.

    Later in the Instagram Story, Jackson is shown expected the moving train as Tori captions the post: "Admiring his hard work."

    The five-year-old was then seen acting as a bit of a dare-devil, placing his foot on the track just to move it away as the train sped past.

    Tori previously shared a series of photos that depicted the process of the family retrieving and setting up their Christmas tree on her Instagram Stories earlier this month.

    The first showed her husband, Zach, and their son Jackson, five, smiling at the camera as the pair visited a local Christmas tree farm.

    She captioned the post: "Sis and I (and Josiah) weren't up for tree hunting today but these boys got a good one!!"

    The "Sis" Tori is referring to is of course her and Zach's middle child, daughter, Lilah, three.

    The next post featured a Boomerang video of Jackson attempting the saw down their chosen pine tree.

    The TLC star explained: "Jackson helping cut her down!"

    The third InstaStory entry showed the bundled tree inside the family's spacious living room.

    Jackson, Lilah, and six-month-old Josiah are pictured in the background as they waited for the tree to be decorated.

    A later video, captioned "Josiah supervising," shows the youngest Roloff watching his mother sweep stray pine needles from underneath the tree.

    It's quickly followed up by a video of the fully decorated tree.

    Capturing the moment, Tori wrote: "I'm exhausted now. But it was worth it."

    HOLIDAY FAIL

    Audrey and Jeremy didn't seem to have as much luck, as fans mocked them after their massive Christmas tree fail.

    The married couple took to their respective Instagram's to show off their oversized Christmas tree, explaining their decision to go so big.

    In Jeremy's Instagram video he shared with fans that he was figuring out how to fit the 16-foot tree into the family's new $2M farmhouse.

    Jeremy and his wife Audrey decided to get a huge Christmas tree for their three children, Ember Jean, 5, Bode James, 2, and Radley Knight, 1.

    "Well, as a lot of things go around here, we decided last minute to get a monster tree - because why not. The kids aren’t getting younger," Jeremy wrote in his Instagram video caption.

    Once Jeremy and Audrey decided to get the tree it then became Jeremy's mission to figure out how to get it set up in their home.

    "We ended up with a 16ft’r and now I need to figure out how I’m going to manage a several hundred-pound tree and stand it up safely," Jeremy wrote.

    TOO MUCH TREE

    Many fans are unimpressed by the Roloff's Christmas tree and are poking fun at them for having such an enormous tree.

    "Your kids aren't going to remember you got a 16 ft tree," one Reddit user wrote.

    "They make everything so much more difficult than it has to be. Just get a normal size tree for the love of God."

    Other fans insinuated that Audrey and Jeremy's Christmas tree choice was more about them than their kids.

    "The kids are going to remember mom’s narcissism and how their parents made everything more difficult than it has to be," one person wrote.

    Some fans even called out the Little People star couple for finding a tree that could pose a danger to their family.

    One critic wrote on Reddit: "So stupidddd. The kids are gonna end up getting hurt from that f**kin thing."

    Another posted: "This is giving me anxiety knowing they have little kids running around, I hope they are careful about that."

    A third urged the reality stars to take safety precautions, such as putting up a baby gate around the tree.
