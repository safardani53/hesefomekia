    What happened to Kodak Black?

    Around 2.45am on Saturday morning, a shooting took place outside of The Nice Guy restaurant in Los Angeles, California.

    Inside the bar, Justin Bieber was hosting a star-studded event ahead of the 2022 Super Bowl.

    In attendance was rapper Kodak Black, age 24.

    After a fight broke out outside of the venue, shots were fired and four victims - all male - were struck, according to LAPD.

    One of the wounded was Kodak Black, whose real name is Bill Kapri.

    With no life-threatening injuries, all three are expected to make a full recovery.

    The investigation remains ongoing.

    Gunna just released DS4 album

    Gunna’s fourth and final instalment of the Drip Season series was released on Spotify on Friday, January 7, 2022.

    The announcement came via a 46-second video posted on his Instagram on New Year's Day.

    The clip showed footage of Gunna partying with friends and was accompanied by the caption, “DS4 1-7-22 #HNY !!” along with a series of emojis.

    The preview included a collaboration track from the album featuring Future and Young Thug.

    American architect Daniel Arsham created the cover art which features a bust of Gunna’s face, complete with a chain and sleek shades.

    Kodak Black filed lawsuit against prison bureau

    The rapper has had a troubled time in prison.

    In September 2020 the Atlanta Black Star reported that Black had filed a lawsuit against the Federal Bureau of Prisons. He claimed prison guards at Big Sandy Penitentiary in Inez, Kentucky, had beat, tortured and mentally abused him since his arrival.

    He was moved to FTC Oklahoma City soon after, then to Thomson some time following that. 

    Around this time he publicly asked Donald Trump to commute his sentence.

    Criminal history, continued

    Black also pled guilty to second-degree criminal possession of a weapon in March 2020, following an April 2019 arrest at the border between the United States and Canada.

    The sexual assault charge that still stands against Black related to an incident in Florence back in 2016.

    According to the Florence County Public Index, Black’s case is still moving through the court system. If charged, he could face up to 30 additional years in prison.
