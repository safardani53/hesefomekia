    I’M on a first date, we are both completely naked and I have not even bought her a drink yet.

    No, I am not a modern-day Casanova — I am at a speed-dating event with a difference.

    We are all completely starkers.

    Run by match-making company Date In A Dash — and inspired by Naked Attraction on Channel Four — singletons strip off before moving around the room in a series of four-minute dates.

    On arrival at The Exhibit bar in Balham, South London, I meet Rob, who runs the event.

    And he seems as stressed as me. The problem: There are 24 men signed up for tonight but only ten women.

    He tells me: “To be honest, it’s a bit of a bloke fest.

    “I think men are just perverts and like to look at naked women. It is always harder to get women to come along.”

    He is forced to split the blokes into two groups of 12 and send half away until later in the evening.

    My group are first up. We are packed off into a room to strip — and are given a skimpy robe to slip into until the big moment.

    Next, we queue outside the function room upstairs where ten single women wait on the other side.

    I ask the chaps if they are feeling nervous — while I gulp at my double whisky — but am met by a chorus of shaking heads.

    For my part, I am extremely on edge at the prospect of spending the evening trying to be charming in my birthday suit — but I have to grin and bare it, literally.

    One guy says: “I’m actually quite excited.

    “I’ve been to hot yoga all week to try and get in shape for tonight.”

    The man next to me is stamping his feet and rubbing his hands together.

    He turns to me and says: “It is a bit cold in here, isn’t it?”

    Excuses, excuses.

    Organiser Rob then gives us a quick pep talk before he opens the door.

    “If you do get aroused, don’t shout about it. Just be discreet and think other thoughts.”

    Then it is time.

    We file into the room in our robes, looking like hotel guests startled by a midnight fire alarm.

    One guy still has his socks on, thick grey woollen numbers bunched up at the ankle. The women are all sat behind small tables, arranged like school desks.

    They are concealing their modesty beneath silky blue dressing gowns. One by one we take a seat opposite a woman.

    I awkwardly introduce myself to the blonde Iranian beauty facing me, but I haven’t even thought about my opening line — I was too focused on the big reveal about to come. I settle for: “This is a bit weird, isn’t it?” She laughs and doesn’t seem nervous in the slightest.

    At the back of the room, Rob blows the first whistle.

    We all stand up, glance about the room self-consciously and disrobe. For a split second there is silence.

    It is like a budget version of the racy Tom Cruise and Nicole Kidman movie Eyes Wide Shut, but with the stately home swapped for a function room above a South London bar.

    But then everyone sits down and the room erupts with excited chatter. Being completely naked is quite a good ice-breaker, it turns out.

    Each date lasts four minutes, then Rob blows his whistle. The women stay seated while the men move around, either wrapping the robe around their waist (like me) or letting their chap hang out for all to see.

    I meet all sorts of interesting and attractive women — one works in recruitment, another in the Department for Culture, Media and Sport and one is a struggling actress.

    My fifth date confides in me that there is another male journalist in the room, a financial reporter who is looking for love in the buff.

    My fourth date tells me she signed up after growing tired of online dating apps Tinder and Bumble. She says: “This just seemed like something a bit different.

    “You have a better chance to get to know someone rather than exchanging a few messages online.”

    Another says: “I’ve been single for four years. It’s time I tried something new.”

    One girl, completely nude of course, stands up as I approach her table.

    I offer my hand out to shake hers but instead she embraces me in a hug.

    There is no getting away from the fact it is a weird way to spend a Thursday night, but the actual dating was a good laugh and at least there were no awkward silences.

    I said “Yes” to seven women.

    The next day I received the news that I had matched with six. And one said she liked me, but just as a friend. It certainly put a spring in my step.

    Maybe I’ll meet one of my fellow naked daters for a drink one evening — clothes on of course. And if things go well, it would be a hell of a story to tell the grandchildren.
