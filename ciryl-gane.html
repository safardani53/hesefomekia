    Early Life 

    Gane was born on 12 April 1990, under Aries’ sun sign in La Roche-sur-Yon, France. He is quite private with his early life and not much information is available.

    Ciryl was the second child of Romain Gane and Maryse. His father, Romain, was a bus driver and soccer player in the Division d’Honneur. He also has an elder brother.

    Ciryl’s nickname was “Good kid” as a child, and he played basketball in his early days. 

    Age, Height, & Body Measurements

    Ciryl Gane is a heavily built fighter with toned abs and tan skin. He towers at 6 feet 4 inches (1.93 meters) and weighs 112 kg (248 pounds).

    He has a light presence of hair, which he prefers to have neatly shaved.

    Talking more about his body features, he has black eyes. Gane has a tattoo inked right in the center of his chest.

    Introduction To MMA 

    Ciryl Gane’s love for the MMA didn’t sprout early in his childhood, unlike other fighters.

    Apparently, he only discovered the passion a few years ago, and before getting involved in MMA, he was first featured in Muay Thai. 

    “I’ve only been interested in combat sports for few years. Before I was in basketball, I was studying parallel studies in BTS management. It was complicated.”
    -Ciryl Gane

    Ciryl Gane was a two-time national Muay Thai champion before his involvement in the MMA. Apart from it, he is also an undefeated player of Muay Thai with a career record of 12-0.

    During that tenure, Gane met Fernand Lopez, who introduced and coached him for the MMA. After that, he entered the MMA and made his debut on 2nd August 2018.

    Gane had a successful debut at TKO Fight Night 1 against Bobby Sullivan as their match took place in Montreal, Quebec, Canada, where they fought for the vacant TKO heavyweight championship.

    The French professional claimed the title with submission in the first round by a front choke.

    Similarly, before moving into UFC, Gane had a total of three matches in TKO, including his debut match. All three matches resulted in his favor.

    His second fight was against Adam Dyczka (win in the second round via a TKO), followed by the match against Roggers Souza (win in the first round by a TKO).

    Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC)

    Just within a year into MMA, Ciryl Gane gained his UFC contract and stepped into it for more fights. Altogether, his promotional debut came on 10 August 2019 against Raphael Pessoa. 

    Their fight was during the UFC Fight Night: Shevchenko vs. Carmouche 2 in Montevideo, Uruguay. Gane claimed the victory via an arm-triangle choke at the end of the first round.

    “After only one year of competition, I signed for the UFC, which had spotted me, and that changes everything in terms of reputation, salary. Now, I can finally say that I live from my sport and my job.”
    -Ciryl Jane 

    Ciryl Gane vs. Don’Tale Mayes

    On 26 October 2019, Ciryl Gane and Don’Tale Mayes provided quite an explosive clash at UFC Fight Night 162 in Singapore Indoor Stadium, Kallang, Singapore. 

    The bout lasted till the third round, where Gane took the victory via a heel hook. All in all, the fight earned him a Performance of the Night bonus.

    Ciryl Gane vs. Tanner Boser

    Following his glorious streak, Ciryl Gane faced Canadian Tanner Boser on 21 December 2019.

    Their bout took place in Busan, South Korea, at UFC Fight Night: Edgar vs. Korean Zombie. Additionally, their match was held live on the Prelims. 

    Gane is bigger than Boser, and they both showcased their own skills. As Gane portrayed his power, Boser showed his technique, changing stances and throwing more punches.

    The match stood aloof till the third round; however, Gane’s power strikes did quite a lot of damage to Boser.

    In the end, Gane claimed the victory by unanimous decision (30-26, 30-26, 30-26).

    Ciryl Gane vs. Junior dos Santos

    Nearly after a year, Ciryl Gane had his fight against Junior dos Santos on December 12, 2020. The match was held in UFC APEX, Las Vegas, Nevada (US), at UFC 256.

    Back then, Santos was having a bad session in his career with 21-8; it was literally like walking into the lion’s den. 

    As the match advanced, Santos tried to get with the outer position while Gane was striking openly from the inner core.

    However, the bout ended in the second round after Gane threw a right hook followed by an elbow putting Santos helpless inside the octagon. 

    Ciryl Gane vs Jairzinho Rozenstruik 

    In a match against Rozenstruik on February 27, 2021, Gane won the match via unanimous decision.

    Ciryl Gane vs Derrick Lewis 

    Gane fought against Lewis on August 7, 2021, at UFC 265 for the Interim UFC Heavyweight Championship.

    In this match, he won the one-sided fight via TOK in the third round. He was also able to win the performance of the night award.

    The professional MMA fighter faced Francis Ngannou for the UFC Heavyweight Championship on January 22, 2022, at UFC 270. Gane was handed his first defeat in any martial arts competition via unanimous decision.

    Ciryl was matched against Tai Tuivasa on September 3, 2022, at UFC Fight Night 209 and won the fight in round three via knockout, which awarded him the Fight of the Night award.

    Shamil Abdurakhimov’s Match And Injury

    Back in 2020, Ciryl Gane’s match with Shamil Abdurakhimov was quite an anticipated one. In fact, they were set to face each other on April 18; however, Gane had to force pull out from the bout. 

    Ciryl Gane struggled with pneumothorax (abnormal collection of air between lung and chest wall or collapsed lung, to put it in simple words) during his training.

    Consequently, this isn’t the first injury Gane had in the UFC. In fact, he has been facing this problem since his early debut in the arena. 

    Thus, Gane took out of UFC 249, and their bout was rescheduled on July 11, 2020, at UFC 251.

    This time, Shamil Abdurakhimov was withdrawn from the bout due to some undisclosed reason.

    Afterward, Gane was scheduled to face Sergei Pavlovich as a replacement for Shamil. However, Pavlovich also pulled out due to an injury.

    Later, Gane and Shamil’s match was fixed for the third time. As per the news, they were to fight on 26 September 2020 at UFC 253. In the bargain, the match was again postponed to UFC Fight Night 180 on October 18, 2020.

    However, Abdurakhimov again pulled out for undisclosed reasons. Therefore, the newcomer, Ante Delija, replaced Shamil. 

    As things don’t end here, Delija also pulled out of the match due to contractual problems with his previous deal with the PFL.

    Ciryl Gane | Junior dos Santos Match

    Controversy 

    As we all know, the glorious victories of Gane and the match between him and Junior dos Santos on December 12.

    The match came out really well on Gane’s side with a technical knockout in the second round. However, Santos had an upset law. 

    After the match, many had controversial thoughts about the ending. Those very thoughts bought up conflicts about the elbow attack of Gane.

    According to the sources, Gane’s elbow attack is considered to be illegal as Santos had backed and turned away from Gane.

    Gane portrayed that attack that landed on the back of Santos’ head. At times, such an attack is quite dangerous and may impact long-term effects; however, neither the referee nor anyone complained about it. Thus, it was considered legal. 

    For that exact match, Daniel Cormier, Joe Rogan, and Joe Anik were on commentary duty, and they also stated that to be fine. Altogether, Gane took the victory over these claims.

    Having said this, Santos was upset and complained about the attack. However, he ended up congratulating his opponent as no one raised a question.

    “The UFC never said a word. It’s fine by them. I find that absurd. A clearly illegal shot, and the president and a bunch of fighters think that what happened was acceptable. Yes, I don’t think it was intentional, but that doesn’t make it any less illegal. I think they’re trying to make this blow over because of the negative point I currently am in my career.”
    -Junior dos Santos

    Legalization of MMA In France

    MMA was not legalized in France. However, right before Gane’s fight with Junior dos Santos, MMA was made legal.

    Obviously, as a Frenchman, Gane couldn’t be any less happy. Alongside the news of the legalization of MMA in France, it also indicated that Gane could be seen fighting by his natives. 

    As an added fact, Ciryl Gane had also depicted his wish to be the first to anchor a show at home.

    “That’s really crazy. MMA started in France. I think in France, we have a big potential for the pay per view, a big potential for the fighters, the French fighters. Of course, this is a big opportunity for me too. And this [would be] a real pleasure, [to fight] in front of my family, of course, my friends and my family.”
    -Ciryl Gane
