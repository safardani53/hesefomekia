    A CAR expert has revealed the best electric cars you can buy for under £20,000.

    Compared to a regular petrol or diesel-powered car, an EV will almost certainly cost you quite a bit more when it comes to signing on the dotted line.

    Handily, car experts at Cinch have listed the cheapest second-hand electric models you can buy in the UK right now.

    Nissan Leaf

    The first mass-produced EV, the Nissan Leaf, was first introduced to the market in 2010 and now spans two generations.

    The experts say: "Nissan’s creation of the e-pedal – one-pedal driving – makes cruising city roads and motorways a breeze, and the quiet engine makes this a comfortable ride for all.

    "Sitting comfortably under the £20k mark, this is a great smaller car for those looking for their daily commute or a change to a more environmentally-friendly style of driving."

    Smart ForFour

    With its distinctive look and the option for a selection of colour combinations, this car screams personality.

    They say: "Smart's ForFour has been compared to models such as the Renault Twingo, providing the best of easy driving while remaining budget- and environmentally friendly.

    "A nifty feature is that it needs only 8.65 metres for a full-circle turn, as its engine sits below the boot.

    "This makes the Smart ForFour the perfect option for anyone who will be driving through small streets regularly."

    SEAT Mii

    The car experts commented that it's similar to the Skoda Citigo and Volkswagen up! - and it's the perfect small car for anyone looking to improve their carbon footprint.

    Car experts at Cinch say: "Designed with flair and featuring zig-zag headlights, this car doesn’t have to be big to make a large impact.

    "The one-litre three-cylinder engine means this car packs a small punch, matching the up! and Citigo, and with some of the newer models, you can expect 161 miles out of this little bargain buy."

    Kia Soul

    The Kia Soul is a compact car with SUV characteristics, and the high driving position allows you to sit tall on the road, giving you excellent visibility and an enjoyable drive.

    They say: "Boasting great features such as a parking aid, sat-nav, cruise control and even a heated steering wheel as an added extra, this car provides great comfort for you and the environment."

    Hyundai IONIQ

    The car experts claim the Hyundai IONIQ is the perfect mix of traditional hatchback beauty and innovative electric engine technology.

    And it was also available as either a plug-in hybrid or fully electric car.

    They commented: "Silent power and smooth pull-away are key comfort indicators of this car’s design, and a cool and modern interior with blue inserts against a dark dashboard makes for an elegant journey.

    "Sitting under the £20k mark, the Hyundai IONIQ is perfect for anyone looking for a sleek electric car."
