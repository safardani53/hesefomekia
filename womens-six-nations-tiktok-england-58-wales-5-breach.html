    26 Mar 2024, 00:00

    ENGLAND boss Simon Middleton reckons a radical half-time shake-up fired his ruthless Red Roses to a thumping 58-5 TikTok Women’s Six Nation win over Wales.

    A bumper crowd of 14,689 flocked to Kingsholm to watch doubles from Lark Davies and Jess Breach cap a rampant display and keep the hosts’ dream of another Grand Slam alive.

    Ioan Cunningham’s side frustrated England in the first half but tries from Davies, Breach, Abbie Ward, Sarah Bern, Alex Matthews, Shaunagh Brown, Emily Scarratt and captain Sarah Hunter helped them cruise to victory.

    And Middleton, 56, said: “I was really pleased with the girls in the second half.

    “It was exactly like we thought it would be. It was tough, physical and hugely competitive.

    “We still had some good moments in the first half, but second half we just went through one or two things that we thought we needed to do to get it back on track - and we put them into practice really well.
