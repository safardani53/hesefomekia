    Little People fans share theory Matt Roloff's girlfriend Caryn forced him to sell farmhouse instead

    LITTLE People, Big World star Matt Roloff's girlfriend, Caryn Chandler, has fans convinced that she pushed him to sell the family's farmhouse. Roloff Farms has been the center of the family's tension since patriarch Matt decided to put the property on the market.

    07 Feb 2024, 00:00

    LITTLE People, Big World star Matt Roloff's girlfriend, Caryn Chandler, has fans convinced that she pushed him to sell the family's farmhouse.

    Roloff Farms has been the center of the family's tension since patriarch Matt decided to put the property on the market.

    The 60-year-old has expressed his desire to pass along his farm duties, so he could retire and move to Arizona.

    However, some fans think the idea was more Caryn's than his after Matt turned down offers from his twin sons, Zach and Jeremy, 32.

    In a popular LPBW forum, one user shared a screenshot of the property's former listing on Zillow.com and pointed out the estimated value for the farm to be $2.4million.

    Matt initially listed the property at $4million - a price tag his son's couldn't match - before taking it off the market in October.

    In the thread, fans theorized that Caryn played a role in selecting the listing price and "influenced" Matt to sell.

    "Caryn comes across as money hungry... Something is off about that woman. Then again she is with Matt, so birds of a feather…." one person commented.

    "Crazy that Amy [Roloff] sold it to him for under $1M assuming it was going to one of her kids and Matt tried to make millions off of it. How much influence you think Caryn has over him?" another wrote, mentioning Matt's ex-wife, Amy, 60, who sold him her portion of the farm in June 2019.

    While Caryn might not be popular with fans, Matt's children have also expressed disapproval of the TLC star's girlfriend.

    FAMILY TENSION

    After their offer to purchase the farm was declined, Zach and his wife, Tori, 31, moved to Washington, but tension still loomed between them, Matt and Caryn.

    During a previous episode of LPBW, Matt said: "A few weeks ago, Zach came to me and said, 'I'd like to submit an offer.' We sat face-to-face, and it didn't go well. There's been some tension between us."

    Caryn, who show viewers slammed for interfering in the drama, noted to Matt: "Now it seems the decision is done.

    "I didn't need to be in the meeting, but… I thought I'd go along. I didn't think there were sides, it was a business transaction, and it didn't work out."

    Fans were stunned to see Caryn step in so heavily, as many felt that she appeared "excited" with the negative outcome.

    Things have gotten so bad that Zach and Tori have not allowed Caryn to meet their newborn son Josiah.

    CARYN QUITS

    Meanwhile, The U.S. Sun revealed Caryn had quit the long-running series.

    "Matt really wants another season, but Caryn is completely done," the source exclusively told The U.S. Sun.

    "She thinks there are too many disagreements with what they want to say and do on air, and too much family drama.

    "She feels as though she's the one always caught in the middle trying to mediate."

    "Although she knows it probably makes for good ratings, she doesn't want to be a part of it anymore," the insider added.

    The U.S. Sun also revealed Caryn is planning to move to Arizona full-time by early next year.
