    Barry Eugene Carter, better known by the stage name Barry White, is an American singer-songwriter, musician, record producer and songwriter. A two-time Grammy winner known for his deep baritone voice and romantic image, his greatest success came in the 1970s as a solo singer and with The Love Unlimited Orchestra, creating numerous songs. Soul, funk and disco are as enduring as his two biggest hits: "You are the first, the last, my everything" and "You can't get enough of your love, baby". Due to his large face, hairy face and deep voice, he was given the nickname "The Hippo of Love". White recorded 19 studio albums throughout his career, but several versions and synthesizers released around the world were certified gold, 41 of which also reached platinum. White has 20 gold singles and 10 platinum records, with worldwide sales exceeding 100 million, and is one of the best-selling musical artists of all time.
