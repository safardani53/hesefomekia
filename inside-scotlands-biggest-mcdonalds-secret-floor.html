    THERE'S one iconic city centre restaurant you won't need to book a reservation for in advance.

    This Glasgow eatery does exactly what it says on the tin.

    Whether you've just finished a nightshift, spilled out of a nearby nightclub or are simply hanging around the Four Corners for a taxi - McDonalds on Argyle Street is a melting pot of characters.

    And we can reveal this burger joint is the biggest in Scotland - and foodies are lovin' it.

    With two floors, and a gross floor space of 1194 square metres, the Big Macs are flying out the door.

    The fast food franchise has an impressive 294 seat capacity and has been feeding hungry foodies for decades.

    With the Golden Arches of Motherwell, Lanarkshire - only 15 miles away - not far behind with an impressive 211 seats.

    However, die-hard McNugget fans might be shocked to discover the Glasgow eatery boasts a hidden third floor - out of bounds for burger boffins.

    It's understood the hidden top level was formerly used for staff training and is still owned by the franchise.

    And the supersized McDonalds joins a host of other massive establishments dotted across the country.
