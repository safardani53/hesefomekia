    AN EASTER EGG hunt is one of those Easter traditions that we just can't get enough of - whether we're 4 or 44.

    When you have kids in the house, organising everything you need for an Easter egg hunt is essential: it's an afternoon activity for everyone to get involved in.

    You don't need outdoor space to have a successful Easter egg hunt - but you do need a few essential bits of kit.

    Eggs are the most crucial component - you can hide chocolate mini eggs, or DIY eggs the kids have spent all week dyeing or stenciling.

    Plastic fillable eggs are also very popular: not only can these be reused year after year, but their bright colours make them easy to spot and they can be filled with little treats inside - key rings, Slinkies, more chocolate.

    To make an Easter egg hunt a success, it takes a bit of planning: signs and clues can really add to the experience as it becomes a structured game, as opposed to your children running around in a wild, desperate-for-chocolate, panic.

    Don't forget a basket for kids to carry all of their treasures - a DIY one is a nice touch that they can spend a morning decorating exactly how they like.
