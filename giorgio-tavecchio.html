    Giorgio Tavecchio | Early Life, Family, & Education

    The famous Placekicker, Giorgio Tavecchio, was born on July 16, 1990, in Milan, Italy, to his parents, Renato Tavecchio and Gabriella Tavecchio. His Father, Renato, is Italian, and his mother, Gabriella, is an Italian-American.

    The Tavecchio family moved worldwide as Giorgio’s mother worked for the American Government. Later, their family settled in San Francisco. 

    Initially, Giorgio loved playing soccer, which was Italy’s primary sport. He stated that his parents always looked for the best schools and soccer clubs/ teams for him and his brother.

    Hence, the Tavecchio family shifted to Moraga and decided to reside there. Giorgio’s parents still live in Moraga, California, to date.

    Education

    As for education, Giorgio attended Campolindo High School and was introduced to the game of football.

    In an interview, he stated that his friend asked if he wanted to be the Campolindo High School football team’s kicker. His mother urged him to try, and hence, he did.

    The next day when he went to practice, he enjoyed kicking the football. He believes that he started playing football by chance and thoroughly enjoyed it. 

    Moreover, for college Giorgio’s initial plan was to attend the University of California, Davis, as they offered him a soccer scholarship.

    But UC Davis was not interested in him as a football placekicker, so he considered other options. Later, he decided to join the University of California, Berkeley.

    Giorgio Tavecchio | College Career 

    Before jumping into Giorgio’s professional career and achievements, let’s look into his college career.

    Freshman Year

    Giorgio started attending the University of California, Berkeley, in 2008, where he competed with Jordan Kay and Seawright for the college team’s placekicker.

    During his first year, he divided time with Seawright and saved all 25 extra point trials and 9 out of 13 field goal attempts.

    Sophomore Year

    During Giorgio’s sophomore year, he faced Vincenzo D’Amato for the placekicking position.

    Amidst D’Amato managing most of the position, Giorgio converted 14 out of 15 extra-point tries and 8 out of 12 field goal efforts.

    Junior Year

    During Tavecchio’s junior year in 2010, he handled the placekicking position and converted 37 out of 39 extra-point attempts, including 11 out of 16 field goal attempts.

    Within Giorgio’s first three seasons, he recorded a field goal percentage of 68% and reached a 75% field goal percentage following his senior year.

    Similarly, in his last collegiate season in 2011, Giorgio handled all placekicking positions again and converted 36 out of 42 extra-point attempts, including field goal attempts- 20 out of 23.

    Senior Year

    During Tavecchio’s senior season, he recorded his best statistical kicking performance while playing against Arizona State with a 47–38 victory.

    During the win against the Sun Devils, Giorgio transformed all four field-goal attempts and all five extra-point attempts.

    His 20 field goals led the California Golden Bears to the 2011 season of the Pac-12 Conference.

    Giorgio Tavecchio | Professional Career

    Pre-draft

    Proceeding into the draft, the NFL Combine didn’t invite Giorgio. Throughout the California Golden Bears pro day, it was raining before performing any drills.

    He kept running his prepared drills. However, he could not show his talents as every NFL scout went into an indoor practice facility.

    Giorgio remained unsigned, going undrafted during the 2012 NFL Draft.

    Later, he attended a tryout for the NFL team San Francisco 49ers, where he was the only kicker available.

    San Francisco 49ers

    Moreover, on August 29, 2012, Giorgio signed a three-year contract worth $1.44 million with the San Francisco 49ers.

    During that time, Brad Seely, special team coach of the San Francisco 49ers, was looking for an additional kicker to succeed the longtime veteran, David Akers.

    Eventually, the coach selected Giorgio as Akers was also a left-footed kicker.

    Seely thought they could use the same holder instead of preparing another holder for a right-footed kicker.

    Green Bay Packers

    Furthermore, after the San Francisco 49ers waived Giorgio during the offseason. The Green Bay Packers signed a three-year contract worth $1.48 million on March 26, 2013. 

    The Packers signed Tavecchio during the offseason to bring competition with Mason Crosby. However, Giorgio was waived by the Bay Packers on August 26, 2013.

    Detroit Lions

    The Detroit Lions signed Girogio for the reserve/futures contract on January 1, 2014. Later, the Detroit Lions waived him after the kicking competition on August 25, 2014.

    Oakland Raiders

    The Oakland Raiders claimed Girogio on August 27, 2014, off waivers. However, after three days, he was waived by the Raiders.

    Moreover, on February 23, 2015, he signed a contract to return to the Raiders, but it was waived again on September 5, 2015.

    Later, on January 5, 2016, Oakland signed a two-year reserve/futures contract worth $990,000 with Giorgio. But, on August 29, 2016, he was released by the team.

    On April 19, 2017, Giorgio signed up with the Raiders and was soon waived by the team on September 2, 2017. Later he was signed up by the team for the practice squad.

    The Raiders promoted Giorgio to the active roster on September 9, 2017, after the Raider’s longtime kicker Sebastian Janikowski, suffered from a back injury.

    He played his first game on September 10, 2017, and scored four field goals on four attempts, with two 52-yard attempts and two extra points during two shots.

    He provided over half the scoring in a 26–16 win while playing against the Tennessee Titans.

    During Week 1, Giorgio tied for the most points scored by a kicker with Greg Zuerlein of the Los Angeles Rams.

    50-plus yard

    Notably, Tavecchio became the first NFL player to hit two 50-plus-yard field goals on his debut.

    Tavecchio’s performance during Week 1 earned him AFC Special Teams Player of the Week.

    Similarly, in the next game against the New York Jets with a 45–20 victory, he scored a season-high six extra points to go along with a field goal of a 29-yard field goal.

    Overall, Tavecchio ended the 2017 season with 33 out of 34 extra-point attempts converted to go along with field goals 16 out of 21.

    The Raiders waived Giorgio after the team signed veteran kicker Mike Nugent on August 3, 2018.

    Atlanta Falcons

    The Atlanta Falcons signed Giorgio on August 27, 2018. However, he was waived by the team on September 1, 2018.

    Later, he was again re-signed by the team on October 10, 2018, after Matt Bryant, an incumbent kicker, injured his hamstring after kicking a 57-yard field goal during the previous Week.

    During Week 7, Giorgio earned an extra point of perfect two for two and field goals of 3 for 3, including a career-long kick of 56-yards, during a 23-20 win against the New York Giants.

    His exceptional performance earned him the NFC Special Teams Player of the Week honors.

    Moreover, on August 31, 2019, Tavecchio was waived by the Falcons after the team re-signed their veteran player Matt Bryant.
