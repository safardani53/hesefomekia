    A SCOTS mum has revealed the cleaning products she swears by when it comes to keeping her home spotless - and the smell transports her on holiday.

    Katlin Duncan, from Glasgow, has been using Spanish brands for her laundry and home since she came across them on TikTok.

    And ever since she got her hands on them, she has decided to sell products from brands such as Asevi, Las 3 Brujas and Nenuco to other cleaning fanatics through her business Soaps & Scrubs.

    Katlin started up her business two years ago selling bath bombs and soap bars as a hobby.

    But now wants to expand her business which she hopes can become her full-time job.

    The mum-of-two told the Scottish Sun: "I came across a TikTok where these Spanish cleaning products and baby bath products were trending and I had to try them out.

    "I managed to order some to try and I loved them.

    "I looked into stocking them in my business so everyone else could get a chance to try them.

    "Everyone gets fed up with using the same old supermarket brands so, I thought it would be a great opportunity and who doesn't want their house to smell like a villa in Spain."

    Katlin swears by the overseas products to keep her home fresh and clean.

    Most read in Fabulous

    But her favourite by far is the Asevi stain remover which has worked wonders on her family's clothes.

    She even tried the product on her partner's work body warmer which was covered in dirt and oil from his job as a mechanic.

    After using the product and putting it in at a 60 degree wash - it came out looking brand new.

    Katlin sells the stain remover for £5.50.

    She also recommends cleaning fanatics try out Las 3 Brujas glass cleaner which she sells for £4.50.

    The Asevi blue floor cleaner and fabric softener are also at the top of Katlin's list - with the floor cleaner priced at £4 and fabric softener at £5.50.

    She added: "I can vouch for the stain remover, it has worked wonders on my kids’ clothing and the fabric softener is out of this world, so different to the usual brands that you would buy.

    "The cleaning products are amazing and honestly smell like holiday."

    Since selling the products within her business, Katlin has been kept busy with orders from cleaning fans wanting to get their hands on the Spanish products.

    Katlin gets her stock from a wholesale supplier and is having to sometimes place two orders a week to keep up with demand.

    She now sells around 50 to 60 products a week - and can sell even more when she sets up a stall at local markets.

    Katlin said: "The reaction has been amazing, I have had loads of people buy them and come back for more."
