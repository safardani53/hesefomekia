    LITTLE People, Big World fans can tie the knot on Roloff Farms for $850 with Zach Roloffs wife Tori as their wedding photographer! Exes Matt and Amy Roloff are offering a two-hour venue rental for 25 guests with a fee of $850.

    23 Sep 2024, 00:00

    LITTLE People, Big World fans can tie the knot on Roloff Farms for $850 with Zach Roloff’s wife Tori as their wedding photographer!

    Exes Matt and Amy Roloff are offering a two-hour venue rental for 25 guests with a fee of $850. 

    The description on the website read: “Roloff Farms in Helvetia is introducing our stunning scenic and romantic outdoor space perfect for couples who want a simple, but beautiful venue for their wedding ceremony.”

    The venue includes two picnic tables, Bride and Groom table with two chairs, round wood table for cake/gifts, portable restroom with hand washing station, free parking, bride staging area, welcome table, chairs for the ceremony, wooden archway, gazebo and more. 

    Decor, flowers, linens and signs must be provided by customers. 

    Customers can also rent a stagecoach for $175, pavilion chairs for $15 each, round tables for $25 each and Helvetia Hard Cider for $100. 

    The in-house photographer is Zach’s wife Tori!

    Tori recently revealed she is starting her own business called Tori Roloff Photography.

    She said on her Instagram Story: “For this event I will charge $600 for 2 hours and post production!”

    She said the reason the maximum is 25 people is because of the coronavirus pandemic. 

    The weddings will also be private. 

    In photos on Roloff Farms, the venue seems to be the perfect spot for a rustic farm wedding. 

    Romantic lights hang from the rustic barn. 

    The gazebo is the perfect spot to say “I do” as loved ones look on. 

    Decor including chalkboard signs, sunflowers, and a two-tier wedding cake help brides and grooms envision their big day.

    The stagecoach is also the perfect prop for photos!

    Matt previously revealed the renovations he is making on Roloff Farms.

    He captioned a series of snaps in May: “After a very productive 3 weeks back at the farm... logs are prepped for new cabin, lawns all fertilized and mowed, new employees hired and trained for summer season, goats and sheep groomed and happy, big house cleaned and painted, birthdays celebrated, grandkids played with... fields plowed.

    “New house designed with permits submitted. Farm is under control. New Covid cleaning procedures in place… Now time to catch my breath… before real summer work begins.”

    One photo shows luxurious modern yet rustic home with a wood-beamed ceiling, decorative stone wall, spacious glass windows and expansive outdoor patio perfect for entertaining.
