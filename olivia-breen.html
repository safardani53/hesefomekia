    Olivia Breen popularly known as Livvy, is a Welsh Paralympian athlete. Breen, who has cerebral palsy, mostly participates in T38 sprint and F38 long jump competitions. She has represented Wales at the Summer Paralympics in 2012, the Commonwealth Games in 2014 and 2018, and she won gold in the long jump at the Gold Coast games in 2018. Her personal bests in the 100m and 200m sprints are 13.34 seconds and 28.07 seconds, respectively. She finished second in the world in the T38 100m sprint and third in the T38 200m sprint.

    Career

    Olivia Breen suffers from cerebral palsy. She has been racing since she was in grade school. She received her T38 classification in January 2012, allowing her to be considered for Paralympic selection.

    In January 2012, she competed in the IPC Athletic European Championships in the Netherlands. She finished third in both the 100m and 200m sprints. She set a personal best in the 100m at the European Championships the same year.

    In July 2012, she set a personal best in the 200m sprint at the Diamond League competition at Crystal Palace in London. She was eventually nominated to Great Britain’s Paralympic athletics team for the Summer Paralympics in 2012. She ran the T38 100m and 200m sprints. She finished sixth in the 100m final with a time of 14.42 seconds.

    With a timing of 30.22 seconds, she finished eighth in the 200m final. She also took part in the first leg of the T35-38 women’s relay team, which won bronze. Following that, she competed in the 2013 IPC World Championships in Lyon. She came in fifth place in both the 100m and 200m. She subsequently shifted from sprinting to long jumping. She competed for Wales at the 2014 Commonwealth Games.

    In 2014, she competed in the IPC European Championships. She took bronze in the 4*100m relay as an individual. She won silver in the T35-28 relay, setting a new British mark of 53.84 seconds. At the IPC European Championships in Grosseto in 2016, the Welsh team won gold in the 4*100m relay. At the 2017 IPC World Championships, she won gold in the Long Jump. She competed in the Commonwealth Games in 2018. She won gold in the long jump and bronze in the 100m.
