    KARDASHIAN fans reveal a new theory explaining why Kim often wears sunglasses indoors and at night.

    The reality star's recent looks have involved sunglasses, including the stunning blue ensemble she donned at the Vanity Fair Oscars Afterparty.

    Fans think they know the reason behind the model's accessory as one Reddit user suspected they are meant to "hide" the results of a cosmetic procedure.

    The poster elaborated on their theory along with the caption: "IMO, Kim wears the sunglasses to hide her recent face work."

    They went on to compare her 2013 makeover when she dyed her hair blonde amid rumors she got a nose job, to her current style of choice.

    "Similar to how she dyed her hair blonde when she got a nose job, I think the sunglasses are to ease the transition.

    Kim denied the nose job allegations back in 2019 saying: "I never had my nose done. Everyone thought I did, and I said, 'Wait until I have kids because your real features come out.'

    "Pictures, I swear, I look at them and would be like, 'Wow, the bump looks so much bigger in some pictures than in others."

    STUNNING IN BLUE

    Kim's streak of pairing sunglasses with her outfits continued over the weekend when she sported silver shades to the star-studded afterparty.

    The 41-year-old wore a skin-tight turquoise garment, which featured cut-out details at the back and a long, dramatic train.

    She styled the dress with pointed black boots slicked her long hair back into a sleek ponytail.

    The mother-of-four went solo on the red carpet, leaving her boyfriend Pete Davidson, 28, at home.

    The couple began dating in October 2021, months after her split from Kanye West, 44.

    REUNITED

    Kim decided to go bare-faced to the game rocking a black hoodie and matching slacks with her hair tied back into a bun.

    The former partner's eight-year-old daughter North also tagged along wearing an oversized sweatshirt, baggy jeans and dreadlocks.

    Kim and Kanye are also parents to daughter Chicago, four, and son Psalm, two.
