    Who was Marianne Ihlen and when did Leonard Cohen write So Long, Marianne?

    30 May 2024, 00:00

    LEONARD Cohen sadly passed away today, the announcement was made on his official Facebook page and the news was confirmed by his son Adam.

    The Canadian songwriter and singer of Hallelujah grew close to Marianne Ihlen back in the 1960s while they were living on the Greek island of Hydra and she became his muse over the next few years.

    Who was Marianne Ihlen?

    Marianne Ihlen was the girlfriend and muse of Leonard Cohen for several years during the 1960s and she was the subject of the famous song "So Long, Marianne".

    The pair met while they were living on the Greek island of Hydra while Marianne was still married to the Norwegian writer Axel Jensen.

    Over the next few years, Marianne inspired Leonard to write a number of songs on his first two albums, Songs of Leonard Cohen (1967) and Songs from a Room (1969).

    Sadly Marianne also passed away in late July this year, aged 81, after a battle with leukemia, her friend Jan Christian Mollestad contacted Leonard to tell him and he wrote a final letter to her.

    It read:

    "Well Marianne it's come to this time when we are really so old and our bodies are falling apart and I think I will follow you very soon.

    Sadly, Marianne died in late July but the couple spent most of the 1960s together and she was Leonard's muse for a number of songs

    "Know that I am so close behind you that if you stretch out your hand, I think you can reach mine.

    "And you know that I've always loved you for your beauty and your wisdom, but I don’t need to say anything more about that because you know all about that.

    "But now, I just want to wish you a very good journey. Goodbye old friend. Endless love, see you down the road. - Leonard Cohen"

    How did they meet?

    Marianne and Leonard met because her husband Axel was also having a relationship with the songwriter's girlfriend Lena.

    Soon after Marianne and Leonard starting living together on the Greek island and a few months later he drove her to Oslo to finalise her divorce to Axel.

    After the divorce, Leonard invited Marianne and her six-month-old son to live with him in Montreal, Canada, which she accepted and they lived together throughout the 1960s, spending their time commuting between New York, Montreal and Hydra.

    When did Leonard write So Long, Marianne?

    Leonard was inspired to write the song So Long, Marianne after he met Marianne during the late 1960s and it featured on his debut album Songs of Leonard Cohen.

    The lyrics to the song read: "Come over to the window, my little darling I'd like to try to read your palm" and the chorus says "Now so long, Marianne, it's time that we began".

    More recently the song made it to number 190 on Pitchfork Media's "The 200 Greatest Songs of the 1960s" list.

    A SECRET CHORD: The incredible success of 'Hallelujah'

    Cohen's 'Hallelujah' was first released on the 1984 album 'Various Positions' and had little success initially.

    However, a popular cover by John Cale and then another by Jeff Buckley propelled it into the public's conscious. It has since been covered an estimated 500 times.
    In an interview with BBC Radio 4, producer John Lissauer said the record company hated it and called it "un-releasable".
    A poll of 50 songwriters by Q magazine had the song as one of the 10 greatest tracks of all time.
    'All Of Me' singer John Legend said Buckley's version is "as near perfect as you can get. The lyrics to 'Hallelujah' are just incredible and the melody's gorgeous and then there's Jeff's interpretation of it. It's one of the most beautiful pieces of recorded music I’ve ever heard."
