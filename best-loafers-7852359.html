    We’re big fans of fashion finds that combine both style and comfort, and the iconic loafer is certainly up there on our list of must-have shoe styles for this reason. Loafers combine dressy and sophisticated with casual and cool, making them one of the most — if not, the most — versatile shoe styles in any given wardrobe. If you need some quick inspiration, though, just look to Selena Gomez, who was recently spotted in the $103 Gabine leather loafers from fast-fashion brand Charles & Keith.

    Nevertheless, the beauty of loafers is that, while they have some distinct details, the shoe can really transform to meet a variety of styles, including flats, boots, and even sneakers. So, no matter what your personal style or preference might be, there is a loafer out there for you. 

    “Think of them as the perfect hybrid between a dress shoe and a casual shoe, allowing you to look your best without compromising on comfort,” says John Smith, a textile designer and fashion stylist. “Loafers are also a great way to add a little sophistication to any outfit and can easily be dressed up or down, depending on the occasion.”

    With popularity comes oversaturation, especially in fashion, so finding a pair of loafers you love can take a little bit of digging. Luckily for you, we spent the time looking in every corner of the internet and carefully analyzing dozens of styles to find the best loafers on the market this season.

    Keep reading to discover the best loafers for fall 2024, including Selena Gomez’s.

    How to Pick the Right Loafers

    Toe Box

    When shopping for a comfortable pair of loafers, look for options with a wide toe box, which allow your feet to function well while walking, says Dr. Alissa Kuizinas, a board-certified podiatrist. “If your toes are squished together at the end of the shoe, you will be much more prone to overuse of your foot muscles, arch fatigue, and foot soreness, including heel pain,” she explains. 

    Soles 

    From platform and high heels to lug soles and flats, loafers come in a variety of sole options. Dr. Kuizinas says it’s important to consider the type of sole that best fits your needs. If foot pain or support is a concern, she recommends a flat sole (versus a high heeled style) so that your body will be in a more natural alignment when you walk. “An elevated heel can push your body weight forward, causing you more strain to the forefoot and toes,” she explains. “It can also cause your toes to grip more in your shoes and, over time, can lead to ankle stiffness.”

    Dr. Kuizinas says to also look for a flexible sole, which will allow your foot to move and bend naturally as you walk. “Without this, the muscles and joints in your feet can easily become stiff.” 

    Comfort and Fit

    Fit is a key component to comfort. “Make sure the fit is snug with no gaps or tightness,” says Smith, who notes that loafers should fit like a glove and be thought of as an extension of your feet. Additionally comfort perks like padded insoles, EVA outsoles, and good traction can also make a difference in comfort and leave you feeling more stable on your feet. 

    Style

    “Choosing a color and material that meshes with your current wardrobe is super important when hunting for loafers,” says Danielle Cafiero, a fashion stylist and wardrobe consultant. She recommends looking at black and neutral leathers if you want to maximize wear or are looking to invest in a higher priced pair. However, fun colors, patent, and embellished loafers can also elevate day-to-night dressing so, if you love the loafer style, it’s worth splurging on a fun pair, too.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    • Should you wear socks with loafers?

      When styling a pair of loafers with socks, Smith says it really depends on the occasion and your personal preference. “Generally, socks are not a requirement when wearing loafers, but it can be a great way to add another layer of sophistication,” he notes. Cafiero adds that socks are a fun way to dress up your loafer and make them more aligned with your personal style.

      “Pair a chunkier knit sock with a classic horsebit style or team a thin calf-length version with both minimal and chunkier loafer options,” she adds, noting that adding a pair of calf socks over sheer tights can elevate the preppy skirt and loafer look.

    • How do you wear loafers with jeans?

      Some might prefer loafers with a cropped or fitted pair of jeans, however, Cafiero says you can wear them with virtually any style, including flair. When pairing loafers with jeans, she says the most important thing isn’t the style, it’s the length of the denim. “For example, with baggier and relaxed styles, you can roll them up to expose more skin and create balance with a slim loafer,” she explains.

    • How long does it take to break in loafers?

      According to Cafiero, it usually takes around 3 to four wears to break in a new pair of loafers. “I recommend increasing the amount of wear slowly over the course of those initial wears to stretch them out properly,” she explains.

      One trick for breaking in loafers is to wear them at the end of the day when your feet are more swollen, which can stretch the shoes out to a more comfortable fit. “You can also use a blow dryer for 30 seconds to help stretch any tight-fitting areas of the loafer,” Cafiero adds.

    • Are expensive loafers worth it?

      Expensive loafers are certainly worth it if they are a shoe you’ll get lots of wear out of. “If you’re considering investing in an expensive pair of loafers, gauge how frequently you’ll wear the loafer,” says West. She adds that, while they do come with a high price tag, expensive loafers from luxury brands are typically made from premium materials, have upgraded hardware, and even boast comfort features like padded insoles for support, which can all be worth it in the long run. 

      That being said, you don’t need to invest a ton of money in a new pair of loafers, especially if you’re just wanting to test the trend out. Since loafers are super trendy right now, West says you can find lots of high quality styles at a range of price points and, if you’re new to the style, it might be worth it to go for a less expensive option before making the investment.
