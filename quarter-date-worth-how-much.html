    COLLECTORS routinely look for rare coins worth a lot of money, and there’s a quarter you could have lying in your spare change worth a whopping $80,000.

    TikTok user TreasureTown has more than 50,000 followers who look to him for content on rare coins and specific errors that could make your spare change valuable.

    Recently, he shared in a video that there’s a quarter you can look for that could be worth $80,000.

    It's Washington quarter with a 1932 date, but there are a few special elements about an example that sold for $82,250 in 2015.

    “To understand why this sold for so much, we’re going to have to dig a bit deeper,” TreasureTown said.

    This particular quarter will feature a D mint mark and was certified in an MS66 grade by Professional Coin Grading Services.

    read more on coins

    This means that the coin was minted in Denver and that it has an above-average quality of its strike and original mint luster, with less than four small contact marks.

    What makes it special?

    First, know that this coin only has a reported mintage of 436,800 – making it a key date in the series.

    In fact, “they are the rarest Washington quarters you can find,” TreasureTown said.

    Heritage Auctions even gave the coin the title as “King of the Washington Quarters” as there are even high sales of the coin.

    Most read in Money

    The date can be found on the obverse side of the quarter under Mr. Washington's neck.

    The D letter mark can be found near the bottom center of the reverse,

    The TikToker went on to say none are graded higher than MS66, which explains the high price tag of $82,250.

    Another has sold for a whopping $89,125, while the highest on record went for $143,750 in 2008.

    But if you happen to have one in circulated grades, they may only be worth somewhere in the hundreds.

    How are coin values determined?

    This particular quarter has such a high value due to its grade and year, but many quarters could attract quite the value due to unintentional errors.

    These happen when the mint incorrectly manufactures a coin, leading to elements that are not supposed to be included.

    However, experts determine a coin's value based on a variety of factors.

    This includes mintage, errors, demand, age, design and condition.

    More importantly, coins are graded on a rigid scale, which is what collectors will pay big bucks for.

    There are five main components when grading scale including strike, surface preservation, luster, coloration and eye appeal.

    Coins are assigned a whole number between 1 and 70, with the highest figure being flawless.

    How to find rare coins

    Make sure to check your spare change for any rare pennies, nickels, dimes and other coins as many of them could sell for thousands.

    Along with errors and grades, collectors may also pay big bucks if a specific coin has a low mintage.

    A great website to check if your coins are worth anything is eBay.

    You just need to search the full coin name, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value”.
