    31 Jan 2024, 00:00

    KAY Burley called out Health Secretary Matt Hancock live on air this morning as he blundered over the rules for Tier 2 drinkers.

    The Sky News host asked whether takeaway meals are allowed to be eaten in wet pubs that don't serve food but Mr Hancock failed to give the simple answer - which is no.

    Kay Burley said: "It's a serious question..it's really important...can I eat a takeaway in a wet pub?"

    Attempting to answer the question, Mr Hancock said: "It's the law. We passed the law in Parliament, we passed it last night. 

    "And it is for the courts to interpret in each individual case but what I'd say is people not only should respect the law..." 

    But the host insisted he wasn't answering the question and that he wasn't going to take everyone to court.

    And even after quizzing the Health Secretary directly again, he failed to give the simple, straight answer.

    Kay Burley then pushed the stuttering Health Secretary, saying: "You don't know, do you?"

    He then responded: "I know that it is a matter for the courts depending on the individual circumstances.

    "Kay, it doesn't matter how many times you interrupt me, I'm going to tell you what the answer is.

    "The courts interpret the law that is set by Parliament for this country.

    FOOD FIGHT

    "The law has been set last night. If people break that law then it's for the courts, based on the individual circumstances, to determine that."

    But the answer to the question should have been a simple no.

    In Tier 2, the rules are clear that alcohol is only served for consumption on the premises as part of a table meal - so that the venue is operating as a restaurant.

    Wet pubs, by their definition, don't serve food.

    So under the Tier 2 restrictions, a wet pub wouldn't be able to open and serve drinks while customers ate a meal which the venue didn't provide.

    If a pub doesn't serve food, they can't serve alcohol to punters.

    Asked whether Deliveroo orders could be sent to pubs to comply with the requirement to have a "substantial meal" in Tier 2 areas, the Prime Minister's official spokesman said customers were banned from bringing in food purchased elsewhere.

    "It's for both customers and venues to act reasonably and to exercise good judgment when adhering to these regulations," the spokesman said.

    "But businesses that do not ordinarily serve food may enter into a contracting arrangement with other local restaurants, for example, in order that they are able to do so and remain open.

    "However, allowing customers to bring food into the premises that has been purchased elsewhere in order to consume alcohol remains prohibited."
