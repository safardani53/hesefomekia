    Michael John Kricfalusi, known as John K., is a Canadian blogger, illustrator and former voice actor / host best known for creating the Nicktoon The Ren & Stimpy Show. . From 1989 to 1992, he was heavily involved in the first two seasons of the show in almost every aspect of the production, including voicing Ren Höek and other characters. Born in Quebec, Kricfalusi spent his childhood in Germany and Belgium before returning to Canada at the age of 7. He gained his skills largely by copying cartoons from newspapers and comic books as a child, and by studying cartoons and their production systems in the 1940s and 1950s. His main influence was Bob Clampett. After moving to Los Angeles in 1978, he collaborated with Ralph Bakshi and worked for Filmation, Hanna-Barbera and DIC Entertainment on various shows.
