    Shocking moment boy, 15, is shot outside McDonald's by motorbike gunman in horrific drive-by attack

    THIS is the shocking moment a 15-year-old boy was shot outside a McDonalds in a drive-by attack. CCTV footage obtained exclusively by The Sun Online shows a moped passenger fire a gun at the teenager in Coventry.

    08 May 2024, 00:00

    THIS is the shocking moment a 15-year-old boy was shot outside a McDonald’s in a drive-by attack.

    CCTV footage obtained exclusively by The Sun Online shows a moped passenger fire a gun at the teenager in Coventry.

    Shots were fired at the boy and a group of young women desperately sprinted away from the scene on Saturday night.

    The victim was rushed to hospital for surgery and his injuries are described as potentially life-changing, but not life-threatening.

    A kebab shop worker told how he was serving customers when shots were fired just three doors down.

    "SCARY"

    He told The Sun Online: "It was pretty scary. We heard the gunshots go off and you could hear screaming coming from outside.

    "The police and the ambulance were on the scene really fast and the whole road was blocked off.

    "We had customers inside when it happened. The bullets could have hit anyone. We had to close early because of what happened."

    A major investigation has been launched into the shooting at 11.10pm, which is being treated as attempted murder.

    A second person, believed to be around 20, who was standing near the teen, was also injured. He has since been released from hospital.

    Det Insp Harjit Ubhi, from Force CID, said: “This was an appalling and reckless attack in a part of the city centre which was busy with people enjoying nights out.

    "APPALLING ATTACK"

    “We are already going through CCTV and identifying witnesses, and are determined to bring the people responsible to justice as swiftly as possible.”

    The city centre terror came five days after a man was slashed with a machete just yards away.

    The 34-year-old victim was attacked outside a KFC branch situated directly opposite the McDonalds branch on Monday.

    Police are still hunting five suspects. It is not known if the two incidents are linked.

    But shopper James Gallagher, 32, said: "You get the odd bit of trouble around here - usually when people have had too much to drink.

    "But to have two violent incidents like these so close together is shocking.

    "It makes you wonder if they're connected in some way."
