    Eleanor Torrey Powell is an American dancer and actress. Best known for machine dances in the music movies of the 1930s and 1940s, Powell began learning ballet at the age of six and had danced in Atlantic City nightclubs before she was a teenager. At the age of sixteen, she began studying hose and began appearing in music magazines on Broadway. She made her Hollywood debut as a prominent dancer in The George White Scandals. She was known as one of Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer's top dance stars during the Golden Age of Hollywood, appearing in a variety of musical vehicles suited to her talent, most notably Born to Dance, 1938 Broadway Melody and Rosalie. In 1965, she was named the world's best dancer by American Dance Masters.
