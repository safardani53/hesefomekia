    PIERS Morgan raged at Greggs today after they unveiled their new diet doughnuts with holes in them.

    The Good Morning Britain host screamed at viewers "you'll still get fat!" on today's show as he laid into the idea.

    The bakery plans to change its displays so that jam doughnuts are surrounded by ring doughnuts.

    And the hole and lack of fillings mean they have fewer calories.

    Throwing his hands in the air, he ranted at viewers: "Here's the thing, it's still a doughnut. You're going to get fat."

    "And yet all you little health warriors go, I'm going to have a McVegan burger for breakfast and a Greggs doughnut with a hole in it and I'm going to lose loads of weight and be healthy.

    "No, you're going to get really fat and bring your death a little bit closer."

    Greggs' CEO, Roger Whiteside, told a childhood obesity conference that ring doughnuts remove some of the calories.

    According to an article in The Times, he said: “The ring doughnuts are between 200 and 300 calories, the ball doughnuts are between 300 and 400 calories.”

    He added: "People like big cakes, not little cakes . . . we know that we shouldn’t be encouraging people to eat large cakes... but the problem is you have to go with demand.”

    The diet doughnut is Gregg's response to a new government obesity strategy, where the food industry has promised to reduce sugar and offer healthier alternatives such as salad or fruit.

    A Greggs spokeswoman, told The Sun the company was “serious about customer health”.

    She said: “Encouraging healthy eating is a key priority for Greggs and is why we have extended our product range to include more lower calorie options.

    "When it comes to sweet bakery these are not diet products but if customers want to treat themselves then we offer more choices at lower calories.”

    "Greggs has also reduced sugar by 20 per cent across its sweet range. For example, sugar in cookies has been reduced by 27 per cent.”

    A Greggs jam doughnut contains 245 calories, 12 grams of sugar and 10 grams of fat.

    By contrast, a Greggs glazed ring doughnut contains 191 calories, 13 grams of sugar, but only 6.4 grams of fat.

    The worst offender calorie-wise of all the Gregg's options is the triple chocolate doughnut which comes in at 341 calories and contains a whopping 29g of sugar.

    The worst for sugar is the Pink Jammie at 32g. It also contains 321 calories, but only 9.3g of fat.

    Gregg's has also been promoting its "balanced choice" options, which are all meals coming in at under 400 calories.
